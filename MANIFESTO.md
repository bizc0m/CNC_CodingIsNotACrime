# CNC_CodingIsNotACrime (CODEX) – DemoForge  
## Manifesto

### A Letter to the Community

If you’re reading this, you already know the feeling.

The moment when the music kicks in.  
The starfield accelerates.  
Raster bars glow.  
A sinus scroller sends greetings to names you’ve never met — yet somehow feel connected to.

That feeling is what DemoForge exists to preserve.

---

## Origins

Let’s be clear from the start.

I am not a developer.  
Not a designer.  
Not an engineer.

I come from the Atari scene — swapper, DCK intro maker, ripper.  
Later, the PC scene: 0Day / 0Sec, and founder of a French team.

Now I’m retired (yes, I buy my licenses now).

But I never left the culture.

I’ve tried to keep it alive through conferences, and even a TEDx.  
Because this isn’t just nostalgia. It’s something else.

---

## First Contact

Back then, tools like DCK (Democonstruction Kit on Atari) allowed me to get close to a dream.

Sure — “real” coders worked in assembly.

But you work with what you have.

Today, I experiment again.  
People call it *vibecoding*.

It started with a small idea:  
building a hidden screen editor for another project.

Then something shifted.

---

## Intent

DemoForge is not just a tool.

It is an attempt to transmit.

To show that the demoscene is not a relic, not a museum piece, but a living language —  
a mix of code, music, constraints, and creativity.

A culture that still has something to say.

---

## Limitations

This project is imperfect.

Built with limited technical knowledge.  
Sometimes clumsy. Sometimes inefficient.

I don’t really code.  
GitHub is still a challenge.

And that’s exactly why this exists.

---

## Collaboration

This project is open by necessity.

Every contribution matters:

- fixing a typo  
- correcting a historical detail  
- adding a reference  
- sharing a link from https://www.pouet.net  
- pointing to a production on https://demozoo.org  
- or writing actual JavaScript  

I redesigned the structure into simple modules.

Not perfect. Not “industry standard.”  
Just something understandable and shareable.

If there is a better way, I’m open.

Just don’t bury it in complexity.

---

## Tribute

DemoForge is a tribute.

To coders who pushed hardware beyond its limits.  
To musicians who made four channels feel infinite.  
To pixel artists who turned 16 colors into entire worlds.  
To groups who spent months crafting something for a single release.

This mattered.

It still does.

## Recognition

In 2021, the demoscene was recognized in France as part of UNESCO’s intangible cultural heritage:

https://www.culture.gouv.fr/thematiques/patrimoine-culturel-immateriel/vivre-le-patrimoine-culturel-immateriel/reportages/la-demoscene-une-culture-libre-a-la-croisee-des-arts-et-de-la-technique

Recognition is not preservation.

Only practice is.

## What This Project Is

A simple HTML demoscene editor:

- Layer-based composition  
- Classic effects (stars, plasma, raster bars, scrollers)  
- Export to standalone HTML or JSON  
- Source code access  
- Local versioning  
- Cultural references and attribution  
- Web fonts and audio integration
